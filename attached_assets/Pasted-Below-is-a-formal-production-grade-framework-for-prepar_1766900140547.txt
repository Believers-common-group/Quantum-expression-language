QEL-BASED LICENSING FOR NCLT RESTRUCTURING

An Observability-First Governance Framework

⸻

1. The NCLT Problem (Structural, Not Legal)

The National Company Law Tribunal already operates on sound legal principles:
	•	judicial supervision,
	•	creditor protection,
	•	statutory hierarchy,
	•	procedural fairness.

What fails in practice is observability, not law.

Structural Gaps in Current Restructuring
	•	Authority is fragmented across RP, CoC, auditors, valuers
	•	Decisions are spread across filings, emails, affidavits
	•	Timelines are reconstructed post-facto
	•	Discretion is exercised but weakly recorded
	•	Courts receive outcomes, not decision lineage

This creates:
	•	litigation drag
	•	credibility disputes
	•	resolution failure
	•	post-approval challenges

QEL addresses none of the law
and all of the observability.

⸻

2. Core Principle: NCLT Restructuring Is a Licensed State of Authority

Restructuring is not business-as-usual corporate action.

It is a court-sanctioned deviation from normal corporate governance.

Therefore:

Every actor in an NCLT process operates under a temporary, scoped, court-derived license.

QEL’s role is to express, bind, and observe those licenses.

⸻

3. The QEL Restructuring License (QRL)

3.1 What a QRL Is

A QEL Restructuring License (QRL) is a machine-verifiable, court-referenced authority envelope that defines:
	•	who may act,
	•	in what role,
	•	under which order,
	•	for what duration,
	•	with what limits,
	•	and with what observability obligations.

It does not grant power.
It records delegated authority.

⸻

3.2 Why a License Is Necessary (Court Logic)

Courts already ask:
	•	Who authorised this?
	•	Under which order?
	•	Was it within scope?
	•	Was it time-bound?
	•	Was it disclosed?

QRL answers these questions natively.

⸻

4. License Classes in NCLT Restructuring

Each license is role-specific and non-overlapping.

4.1 Resolution Professional License (QRL-RP)

Authority Source
	•	NCLT admission order
	•	Insolvency & Bankruptcy Code reference

Scope
	•	Custodianship of debtor
	•	Process administration
	•	Information collation

Hard Limits
	•	No asset alienation beyond approval
	•	No preferential treatment
	•	No discretionary business expansion

QEL Mapping
	•	Actor: did:qrl:rp:<case-id>
	•	Authority: NCLT order reference
	•	Context: court-supervised state

⸻

4.2 Committee of Creditors License (QRL-CoC)

Authority Source
	•	Statutory creditor rights
	•	Court-recognised constitution

Scope
	•	Commercial decisions
	•	Plan evaluation
	•	Voting

Limits
	•	No operational execution
	•	No unilateral modification

QEL Mapping
	•	Actor: collective actor with voting expressions
	•	Consent: weighted, recorded
	•	Evidence: minutes, votes

⸻

4.3 Valuer / Auditor License (QRL-VA)

Authority Source
	•	Court / RP appointment

Scope
	•	Valuation
	•	Audit
	•	Verification

Limits
	•	No decision authority
	•	No plan advocacy

QEL Role
	•	Evidence producer only
	•	High confidence, no authority

⸻

4.4 Management-in-Suspension License (QRL-MS)

Authority Source
	•	Statutory continuation
	•	Court-limited recognition

Scope
	•	Operational continuity
	•	Asset preservation

Limits
	•	No strategic decisions
	•	No asset disposition

QEL Importance
	•	Explicitly marks non-authority
	•	Prevents shadow governance

⸻

4.5 Successful Resolution Applicant License (QRL-RA)

Authority Source
	•	Approved resolution plan
	•	NCLT confirmation

Scope
	•	Transitional actions
	•	Conditional execution

Limits
	•	Subject to milestones
	•	Revocable on breach

QEL Role
	•	Time-phased authority
	•	Milestone-linked expressions

⸻

7. Observability During the Resolution Period

Every major action emits a QEL expression:
	•	valuation report submission
	•	CoC vote
	•	plan modification
	•	objection
	•	delay
	•	emergency approval

This creates:
	•	live auditability
	•	clean judicial record
	•	minimal post-facto litigation

8. Court Interaction Model

What the Court Sees
	•	License scopes
	•	Authority chains
	•	Deviations with context
	•	Timeline integrity

What the Court Does Not See
	•	dashboards
	•	AI scores
	•	internal system logs

This preserves judicial independence.

9. Post-Approval & Transition Governance

Once resolution is approved:
	•	QRLs expire
	•	New corporate licenses activate
	•	Legacy expressions remain immutable

This avoids:
	•	authority leakage
	•	retroactive disputes
	•	shadow continuation
